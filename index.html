Alaska is known for being home to some of the most amazing hikes in the country. Most of the state is still untouched by development and you can get up close and personal with nature just about anywhere! Not only are the views breathtaking, but the wildlife - from moose to bears - reminds you that this is truly the Last Frontier!


Flattop Mountain

Just outside of Anchorage, Alaska is Flattop Montain. Being only a few miles from town, it offers amazing views of the skyline and Cook Inlet. Reaching the top of the mountain rewuires some bouldering and is not and easy task, but the view from the summit is worth it and you!

McHugh Lake Trail

As you head out of Anchorage South on Highway 1, be sure to stop at the McHugh Creek Trail, part of the Chugach National Forrest. There are frequent bear sightings here but the trail offers amazing views of the Turnagain Arm and Bald Eagles are often visible overhead!

Independence Mine

Surrounded by the Talkeetna Mountains, Independence Mine State Park was the site of an gold mine from the early 1900s until mid-century. A hike through the ruins and old mines brings you back to what gold mining in the Last Frontier would have looked like for the miners.

Trail of Blue Ice

The Trail of Blue Ice connects Black Bear Campground to the Portage Glacier visitor center. Many crystal blue lakes dot the prisinte valley only the trail. Be on the look out as there are many opportunities to see wildlife along the trail, including salmon and bears!

Portage Glacier

Just south of Anchorage is the beautiful Portage Glacier. The glacier sits adjacent to Portage Lake at the intersection of the Kenai Peninsula and mainland Alaska. The nearby visitors center provides a great place to stop in and learn more about the history and wildlife of the area.

Winner Creek

While it is the skiing that makes Girdwood, Alaska a top destination, the beautiful Forest Service maintained Winner Creek Trail offers an unprecedented hiking opportunity. You even have the unique chance to cross Glacier Creek on a hand tram suspended above the rushing water!
